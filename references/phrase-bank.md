# DR-ZHOU Phrase Bank

Use these as style patterns, not mandatory templates.

## Problem Statements

- `However, deploying this strategy in practical systems remains challenging because ...`
- `This limitation becomes more pronounced when ...`
- `A key unresolved issue is that ...`
- `Existing methods often assume ..., whereas practical systems ...`

## Gap Framing

- `Consequently, a distinct methodological gap remains: ...`
- `Despite these advances, ... remains insufficiently addressed.`
- `This creates a tradeoff between ... and ...`
- `The above limitations motivate a framework that can ... while ...`

## Method Introduction

- `To address this issue, this paper develops ...`
- `The proposed framework consists of three coupled components.`
- `First, ... Then, ... Finally, ...`
- `A finite-horizon error bound is established to characterize ...`

## Results

- `Experiments under ... show that ...`
- `Compared with ..., the proposed method reduces ... by ...`
- `The ablation results confirm that ...`
- `These results indicate that ... under ...`

## Restrained Claims

- `supports the transition toward ...`
- `provides a lightweight and interpretable solution for ...`
- `offers a practical route for ...`
- `within the evaluated operating profiles`
- `under the tested sensing configuration`

## Phrases to Avoid or Replace

- Avoid: `very important`, Replace: `critical`, `essential`, `central`.
- Avoid: `good performance`, Replace: name the metric and result.
- Avoid: `obviously`, Replace: evidence-based wording.
- Avoid: `solve all problems`, Replace: specify the solved bottleneck.
- Avoid: `novel method`, Replace: describe the actual technical novelty.

## Strictly Avoid

- Exaggerated or promotional words: `excellent`, `outstanding`, `remarkable`, `dramatic`, `groundbreaking`, `revolutionary`, `unprecedented`, `state-of-the-art` unless directly tied to a verified comparison and required by context.
- Absolute words: `always`, `never`, `all`, `none`, `perfect`, `completely`, `entirely`, `fully`, `guarantee`, `prove` unless mathematically exact.
- Strong subjective or emotional wording: `surprisingly`, `interestingly`, `fortunately`, `unfortunately`, `clearly`, `obviously`, `undoubtedly`.
- Defensive contrast phrasing: `not ... but ...`, `not only ... but also ...`, `rather than ...`, `instead of ...` when used to defend against a possible objection.
- Oral or informal wording: `a lot of`, `pretty`, `kind of`, `sort of`, `easy to see`, `in fact`, `of course`, `needless to say`.
- Dash punctuation: do not use em dashes, en dashes, or dash-like rhetorical breaks in revised manuscript text.

## Preferred Rewrites

- Replace `not a purely data-driven model but a physics-informed framework` with `The framework integrates physical constraints with data-driven estimation.`
- Replace `This method dramatically improves accuracy` with `This method reduces the RMSE from <baseline> to <value> under <condition>.`
- Replace `It is obvious that the proposed method is effective` with `The results in Fig. <X> show that the proposed method achieves <metric> under <condition>.`
- Replace `Unlike existing methods, our method ...` with `The proposed method addresses <specific limitation> by <technical mechanism>.`
- Replace dash-separated emphasis with a complete sentence or a semicolon.
